Story

Founded in 2001 in Kinshasa, the Congolese Association of Customs Brokers and Logistics (ACCDL) works to promote and support logistics experts in the Democratic Republic of Congo.

Who Are We?

The ACCDL is a professional organization dedicated to the promotion of logistics and international trade by supporting customs brokers and logistics experts. We work closely with the Directorate General of Customs and Excise (DGDA) to ensure high standards of professionalism and efficiency in our country's logistics sector.

  • Professional Support

    Provide training and certifications to improve the skills of logistics experts.

  • Promotion of Logistics

    Raise awareness and promote best logistics practices through seminars, conferences, and publications.

  • Strategic Partnerships

    Collaborate closely with the DGDA and other stakeholders to improve customs and logistics processes.

  • Expert Directory

    Maintain an up-to-date list of logistics experts accredited by ACCDL.

frequently asked questions

here is the list of different frequent questions

Can you explain to me the customs clearance process for the goods I wish to import?

Quels sont les documents requis pour importer des produits électroniques dans ce pays ?

The customs clearance process involves several key steps. First of all, you must provide a detailed customs declaration with information about the goods, their value, country of origin, etc. Next, customs officers will carry out a documentation check and may also carry out a physical inspection of the goods. Depending on the type of product, customs duties and taxes may be applied. Customs clearance can take a few days, so it is important to plan your import schedule carefully. Please feel free to ask me further questions if you need more details on the different steps.

How should I go about importing food products into this country?

The importation of foodstuffs is subject to rigorous sanitary and phytosanitary controls. In addition to the usual customs documents, you will need to provide: a certificate of inspection and analysis of the products, a phytosanitary certificate if the foods are of plant origin, as well as an import authorization issued by the competent authorities for Food Safety. Depending on the products, laboratory tests may be required before releasing the goods. It is also important to find out about the regulations on labeling, authorized additives, etc. I advise you to plan these formalities carefully in advance to avoid any delays.

What are the steps to follow to declare medicines in this customs clearance procedure?

The importation of medicines requires special attention due to very strict health regulations. In addition to the usual customs documents, you will need to provide: a medical prescription or an authorization to import medicines issued by the competent health authorities, a certificate of analysis of the medicine(s), as well as a detailed technical sheet on the composition and the properties of the products. Depending on the country, additional checks such as laboratory tests may be carried out before the goods are released. I recommend that you find out about the specific requirements in advance to avoid any delays or problems with customs clearance.

What are the particularities to take into account when importing pets into this country?

The importation of pets is subject to very strict health and veterinary regulations in order to prevent any risk of spreading disease. In addition to the standard customs documents, you will need to provide: an international veterinary certificate attesting that the animal is in good health and up to date with its vaccinations, an import authorization issued by the competent authorities, as well as proof of identification of the animal. animal (electronic chip, etc.). Depending on the country, additional quarantines or tests may be required. I invite you to find out about the formalities in advance to ensure that your animal can enter without problems.
